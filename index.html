The Amazing Dumbo Octopus

The Dumbo octopus (Grimpoteuthis spp.) is a fascinating deep-sea creature named after Disney's Dumbo because of its ear-like fins. Unlike most octopuses, it doesn't have an ink sac and prefers to drift gently above the seafloor rather than swim. Dumbo octopuses live in some of the deepest parts of the ocean, where the water is cold, dark, and under high pressure. Despite these extreme conditions, they have adapted perfectly, using their large fins to "fly" gracefully through the water. They are peaceful, slow-moving animals that mostly eat small crustaceans, worms, and other tiny sea creatures. Scientists continue to study these unusual animals because they are rarely seen by humans and can teach us more about life in the deep ocean. Their unique appearance and gentle behaviour make them one of the most interesting creatures living beneath the sea.

Interesting Facts

  • Dumbo octopuses can live 1,000 to 7,000 metres below the ocean surface.
  • They use ear-like fins to glide through the water.
  • They do not have an ink sac like most octopuses.
  • They have webbing between their arms, creating an umbrella-like shape.
  • They mainly feed on worms, crustaceans, and small sea creatures.
